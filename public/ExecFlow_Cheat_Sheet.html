When to Zap

Zap every interview call. The EDS generates in ~5 minutes with no effort from you. Even if a candidate doesn't progress, the intelligence is banked for the future.
Don't zap after only part 1 of a multi-part interview. Combine all parts in one Granola note first, then zap once at the end.
Talent mapping (no interview) continues with the current presentation format. EDS/EDC needs a transcript to work from.

Multi-Part Interviews

  • Create the note for the first call as normal
  • When the second call starts, go back to that original note in Granola and resume from there
  • Once all parts are done, zap it once

Granola Title Format
The title is how the system knows what to do. Two prefixes. That's it.
Interview

IV [Candidate Name]

e.g. IV Sarah Chen or IV Sarah Chen PBV VP Sales

Job Spec

JS [Client] [Role]

e.g. JS Pepsi VP Sales or JS BorgWarner Plant Controller

Nothing Between IV and the Name

× breaks matching IV 1 Sarah Chen
IV #1 Sarah Chen
✓ works perfectly IV Sarah Chen
IV Sarah Chen PBV VP Sales

Keyword Matching

The system uses keyword matching to figure out which search a note belongs to. Including the company or role after the candidate name is helpful but optional. The candidate name immediately after IV is what matters most.

Granola Folders

Folders have zero impact on the backend. The zap triggers from "Send to Zapier" regardless of folder. Organise Granola however works best for you. No need to auto-invite the team when creating new folders.
What the AI Uses
Three source layers, each handled differently. This separation is the core product principle.
Transcript
The raw conversation — what was actually said on the call
→ All factual sections: Scope Match, Key Criteria, Comp, Motivation
Enhanced
Granola's AI summary of the call
→ Cross-reference only — used when the transcript is unclear
Manual Notes
Your typed notes before / during the call
→ "Our Take" section ONLY. Never leaks into factual sections

The Quarantine Rule

Write freely. Personality reads, gut feel, priority signals — the system keeps manual notes completely separate from client-facing evidence. Nothing leaks.

Show Evidence. Let Humans Judge.

Evidence sections are factual, from the transcript. "Our Take" is the only section with consultant judgment. Clients see the evidence; you add the lens.

CV & LinkedIn

The system does not pull from CVs or LinkedIn right now. When it mentions companies or achievements, that's because the candidate said them out loud. The transcript is surprisingly rich.

Pro tip: Pasting a candidate's LinkedIn profile into the manual notes section before a call enriches the data. Not required, but a useful habit. Formal integration is on the roadmap.

Job Summary Workflow

  • Title with JS prefix → Zap after the call
  • Two docs land in SharePoint: the Job Summary (client-ready) and a separate DO NOT SHARE internal intelligence doc
  • Open the Job Summary .docx in Word → review and edit
  • Convert to PDF → move to Finalised folder

Invenias Independence

No Invenias? Still zap. The EDS generates and lands in SharePoint regardless. The only thing skipped is the automatic journal link — which can be added later.
Can I rename finalised JS files? Yes. The system used to depend on the filename but now reads from a structured data store. Keep names consistent for your own indexing, though.
The Executive Decision Card
One page. 90 seconds to scan. Evidence first, judgment last.

What's on the Card

♦
Scope Match
Seasoning line summarising the candidate's fit, plus dimension-level match indicators
1
Key Criteria Assessment
Top criteria with evidence + context anchors showing where the achievement happened
2
Compensation & Timeline
Current package, expectations, notice period — structured, not just a total number
3
Why Interested?
Push and pull factors from the conversation
4
Potential Concerns
Development areas or gaps — the single red-adjacent element
★
Our Take
Your voice. Your judgment. Editable, generated from manual notes only

The Core Principle

"Show evidence. Let humans judge."

Evidence sections come from the transcript only. "Our Take" comes from manual notes only. The client sees structured intelligence. You provide the lens.

What's NOT on the Card

No numeric scores No recommendation badges No interview dates No emojis No grey readiness scan

Match scores exist in the data layer but are hidden by default. Toggle on per-search if needed.
